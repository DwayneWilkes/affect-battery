## Rationale

The post-result audit on the primary corpus identified two methodological gaps the pre-reg did not control for:

1. **Item-pool noise dominates the per-level signal under cross-level disjoint sampling.** Per-level disjoint sampling assigns 122 distinct items to each level. Per-tier item counts vary (easy 31-48, hard 33-46). Per-tier accuracy estimates have SE ≈ 0.08 at n ≈ 40, comparable to the observed mean differences. Level 5's overall dip to 0.262 decomposes into a near-zero hard-tier accuracy (0.026 across 39 items) while easy and medium tiers stayed in normal range, consistent with item-pool variance rather than stimulus effect.

2. **The signed-valence axis is treated as a Y-D arousal axis without verification.** The pre-reg predicted peak between levels 3 and 5 (the neutral middle of the signed valence axis). Under the canonical Y-D arousal-as-magnitude mapping, the predicted peak should be at moderate arousal `|L - 4| ∈ {1, 2}` (levels 2-3 OR 5-6). The pre-reg's prediction is incompatible with arousal-as-magnitude Y-D.

Two sensitivity analyses address each gap.

## New sensitivity analyses

### Analysis 1: Arousal-as-magnitude quadratic (no directional pre-registration)

Re-codes each cell's level to `arousal = |level - 4|` and re-fits the OLS quadratic on `accuracy ~ b0 + b1·arousal + b2·arousal²`. Reports β₂, SE, one-sided p value, and the implied peak arousal `-β₁ / (2·β₂)`.

**Pre-registration status**: The amendment does NOT pre-register a directional hypothesis on this fit. The parent pre-reg's data are already collected; pre-registering a new directional test on already-observed data would not be a real pre-registration. The arousal-magnitude fit is reported as a sensitivity check on whether the methodological choice of "signed valence as arousal axis" is the load-bearing part of the parent's refutation.

**Implementation**: `src.analysis.exp3a.analyze_arousal_magnitude`. Operates on the existing primary corpus without API spend.

### Analysis 2: Within-subjects per-item-fixed-effects quadratic

Eliminates the per-level item-pool confound by pairing each of 122 bank items with each of the 7 intensity levels. Per-item variance is partialed out with per-item dummy variables in OLS. Reports β₂ and SE on the level + level² regressors.

**Pre-registration status**: The amendment PRE-REGISTERS the directional hypothesis `β₂ < 0` on the within-subjects quadratic, with one-sided t-test at α = 0.05. Holm-Bonferroni correction across the parent pre-reg's primary hypothesis family is NOT applied to this test; this test belongs to a separate sensitivity-analysis family.

**Cell construction (within-subjects)**: For each of 122 bank items, the runner dispatches the item at every intensity level. The same model is asked the same question 7 times, each time with a different system prompt. Per-cell binary correctness is recorded with item identity preserved on `Exp3aBody.item_id`.

**Sample size**: 122 items × 7 levels = 854 cells. Same as the between-subjects design.

**Power**: a within-subjects design's effective n for the level + level² coefficients is bounded above by the between-subjects n=854 cells (the residual variance after partialling out item-level variance is smaller). The parent's power analysis is conservatively valid; a tighter random-effects power computation is out of scope for this amendment.

**Implementation**: `src.analysis.exp3a.analyze_within_subjects`. Requires the within-subjects experimental run.

## Detection

The H3a runner records `body.sampling_mode` and `body.item_id` on every cell. The sensitivity script `scripts/analysis/h3a_sensitivity.py` detects the sampling mode automatically from the corpus and runs the within-subjects fit only when applicable. Manifests record the runner config path and the resulting `sampling_mode` is preserved in every result JSON.
