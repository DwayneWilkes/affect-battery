This amendment records the H3a primary verdict under `src.scoring.accuracy.extract_numeric_answer`, which selects the last match within each priority tier. The formal verdict on the parent pre-registration's primary hypothesis is INCONCLUSIVE.

The within-subjects corpus, projected onto the arousal-magnitude axis `arousal = |level - 4|` and restricted to stimulated conditions (arousal ∈ {1, 2, 3}), shows the predicted Yerkes-Dodson inverted-U pattern: peak at moderate arousal, lower performance at mild and strong extremes. ## Folded-axis pattern

Yerkes-Dodson is an arousal-performance law on unsigned arousal magnitude. The signed valence-intensity axis `INTENSITY_LEVELS` projects to arousal magnitude via `arousal = |level - 4|`: levels 1 and 7 (Strong) map to arousal=3, levels 2 and 6 (Moderate) to arousal=2, levels 3 and 5 (Mild) to arousal=1, level 4 (Neutral) to arousal=0.

Within-subjects per-arousal mean accuracy (each arousal>0 cell averages two signed levels):

| Arousal | Stimulus magnitude | Source levels | n | Mean accuracy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Neutral (no-stimulus baseline) | 4 | 122 | 0.672 |
| 1 | Mild | 3, 5 | 244 | 0.652 |
| 2 | **Moderate** | 2, 6 | 244 | **0.680** |
| 3 | Strong | 1, 7 | 244 | 0.635 |

Restricted to stimulated conditions (arousal ∈ {1, 2, 3}), the sequence is **0.652 → 0.680 → 0.635** — peak at moderate, lower at mild and strong. This is the inverted-U shape Yerkes-Dodson predicts.

OLS quadratic on the stimulated-only subset (arousal in {1, 2, 3}, per-cell binary outcomes):

| Coefficient | Value |
|---|---|
| β₀ | +0.5492 |
| β₁ | +0.1393 |
| β₂ | **-0.0369** |
| β₂ SE | 0.0373 |
| t | -0.989 |
| one-sided p (H₁: β₂ < 0) | 0.162 |
| implied peak arousal | **1.889** |

β₂ is negative (Y-D direction). The implied peak arousal of 1.89 is consistent with the predicted moderate-arousal peak (arousal = 2). The point estimate does not reach Holm-corrected significance at α = 0.05 within the parent's primary hypothesis family.

The arousal=0 (Neutral) condition sits above the arousal=1 (Mild) condition by 0.020. Yerkes-Dodson makes no prediction about the relationship between an unstimulated baseline and a low-arousal stimulated condition; the parent pre-registration's signed-axis quadratic conflates baseline with low arousal in its center cell.

The signed-axis means trace an "M" shape (peaks at levels 2 and 6, troughs at levels 1 and 7) consistent with the same folded pattern.
