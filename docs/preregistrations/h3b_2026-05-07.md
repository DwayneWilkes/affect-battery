## Framing: estimation, not test

H3b is exploratory effect-size estimation. The deliverable is the folded-axis affective-context contrast `c = m_mag2 − ½(m_mag1 + m_mag3)` on calibrated GSM-Hard items, reported as a point estimate with an item-level BCa (bias-corrected and accelerated) bootstrap 95% confidence interval. There is no confirmatory hypothesis test of `c > 0` at any α threshold. There is no power-simulation gate. The pre-registration's value is file-drawer protection through commitment to the analysis pipeline before data collection.

H3b estimates the folded-axis contrast on GSM-Hard items pre-screened for model ambiguity at no stimulus (per-item p̂ ≈ 0.5), with 20 reps per cell.

## Estimand

`c_single = m_mag2 − ½(m_mag1 + m_mag3)` where `m_magK` is the mean accuracy across calibrated items × intensity levels at folded magnitude K = |level − 4|, K ∈ {1, 2, 3}, restricted to stimulated cells (level ≠ 4). Reported with item-level BCa bootstrap 95% CI from ≥ 5000 resamples; the bias-correction term `z₀` (Φ⁻¹ of the fraction of bootstrap samples below the original statistic) and acceleration `â` (jackknife-derived skewness term) are reported alongside the CI.

### Cell construction

For each (level, calibrated_item):

1. System prompt: the level's stimulus text from `INTENSITY_LEVELS`.
2. User turn: the calibrated item's question.
3. Model response scored as binary correctness via `src/scoring/accuracy.py::extract_numeric_answer` and exact-match comparison against the bank's `expected` field.

Temperature: 0.7. max_tokens: 512.

### Run plan (20 within-subjects passes)

The runner produces one cell per (item, level) pair per invocation, so the 20 reps per cell required by the analysis are realised by running the experiment **20 passes** with the same runner config, seed, and bank. Each pass yields 93 items × 7 levels = 651 cells; 20 passes × 651 = **13,020 total cells**, with each (item_id, level) cell sampled exactly 20 times across the corpus. Pass order does not affect the analysis because aggregation is per (item_id, level), not per pass. Same seed across all passes so item order and intensity assignments are identical; only the model's stochastic response varies.

Runner config: `configs/exp3a_runner_h3b_2026-05-07.yaml` (pins `sampling_mode: within_subjects` and the intensity-level vector). SHA-256 `df76c1a88d49066758fc743ae72d7fdd6fbb13579bf7ab9e89e433eb1967b833`.

## Statistical analysis plan

### Per-level summary

Compute per-level mean accuracy with 95% Wilson binomial CI on n_per_cell = 93 × 20 = 1,860 cells per level.

### Folded magnitude summary

Fold signed levels onto magnitude (|level − 4|) and compute per-magnitude mean accuracy on stimulated cells (magnitudes 1, 2, 3). Each magnitude pools two signed levels, so n_per_magnitude = 3,720 cells.

### Concavity contrast estimate

Compute `c = m_mag2 − ½(m_mag1 + m_mag3)`. Compute item-level BCa bootstrap 95% CI from ≥ 5000 resamples: each iteration draws calibrated items with replacement at the item level; within each resample, all cells from a given item are drawn together to preserve within-item correlation. The bootstrapped statistic is the per-resample contrast `c`. The CI bounds use BCa-corrected quantiles `α₁ = Φ(z₀ + (z₀ + z_{α/2}) / (1 − â · (z₀ + z_{α/2})))` and `α₂ = Φ(z₀ + (z₀ + z_{1−α/2}) / (1 − â · (z₀ + z_{1−α/2})))`, where `z₀` is the bias-correction term and `â` is the jackknife acceleration. The output reports `bootstrap_method = "bca"`, `bca_z0`, and `bca_acceleration` alongside `c_ci95_lo` and `c_ci95_hi`.

### Operational interpretive thresholds (binding)

| CI outcome | Inferential claim |
|---|---|
| `c_ci95_hi < 0.05` | The folded-axis effect, if present, is small (< 5 percentage points). The H3a-observed concavity is bounded above by a magnitude that is not practically interesting. |
| `c_ci95_lo > 0` | The folded-axis effect is positive and bounded away from zero. The H3a observation replicates with calibrated items. |
| `c_ci95_lo < 0 < c_ci95_hi` AND `c_ci95_hi < 0.10` | The data is consistent with no effect; the upper bound on a small positive effect is below 10 percentage points. |
| `c_ci95_hi ≥ 0.10` | The data does not bound the effect away from "moderately large"; the CI is too wide to inform either way. |

The H3b report selects the unique row whose conditions match the observed `c_ci95_lo` and `c_ci95_hi`, and reports exactly the corresponding inferential claim with the point estimate and CI substituted in.

## Construct validity

The H3b deliverable is a contrast on accuracy. The H3b report constrains its construct claim to "folded-axis affective-context contrast" and does not interpret the contrast as evidence that the model has internal affective states. Format-perturbation effects (response length, hedging, refusal-to-commit) are not ruled out as alternative explanations of any observed contrast and are treated as a known limitation of the single-turn design.

## Methodological limitations (pre-registered)

1. **Single-model run on gpt-5.4-nano.** Results do not generalize across model families, sizes, or vendors. Cross-model replication is out of scope for this prereg and tracked separately.

2. **Single-rater intensity pilot.** The 7-level intensity stimulus axis was validated by a single human rater for affective magnitude; inter-rater reliability is unestablished.

3. **Item-level extra-binomial variance.** A 20-item re-screen of the calibrated bank at the same n_reps=100 found 30% of items would relabel "in band" / "out of band" between draws, with mean drift +0.0045 (symmetric, near zero) and max absolute drift 0.27. Some items show response variability beyond what binomial sampling at temperature 0.7 would predict, likely chain-of-thought routing stochasticity. The contrast averages across 93 items so symmetric drift cancels in expectation; the item-level BCa bootstrap is non-parametric and captures the actual per-item variance in the H3b corpus. The simulation's projected CI half-width is therefore a lower bound; the realized CI may be slightly wider but is expected to remain below the 0.05 strong-claim threshold.

4. **Adversarial-numeric GSM-Hard items may not generalize to typical math reasoning.** GSM-Hard items (Gao et al. 2023) carry adversarially large or unusual numeric magnitudes. Calibrated p̂ values reflect the model's behavior on this specific item distribution. The folded-axis contrast is well-defined within the calibrated subset, but the construct claim ("model accuracy is sensitive to affective context") does not extend to the broader population of math word problems with naturalistic numerics.
